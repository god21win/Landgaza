Chapter One
HOW IT STARTED — THE ORIGINS
Historical Context

SEVENTY-SIX YEARS
OF DISPOSSESSION

The current catastrophe did not begin on October 7, 2023. It is the culmination of more than seven decades of displacement, occupation, and siege — a history that shaped every dimension of what Gaza's children face today.

Gaza is one of the most densely populated territories on Earth — 2.3 million people, more than half of them children, packed into 365 square kilometres. Before October 2023, more than 70% of Gaza's population were refugees and descendants of refugees from the 1948 Nakba — families expelled from what is now Israel and who have never been permitted to return.

Since 2007, Gaza has been under a comprehensive land, air, and sea blockade. The blockade controls the movement of every person and every kilogram of goods — food, medicine, fuel, building materials — that enters or leaves. Before the current war, 80% of Gaza's population depended on humanitarian aid to survive. The blockade created the conditions; the bombs completed them.

What has happened to Gaza's children is not a natural disaster, not an unfortunate side-effect, not collateral damage. It is the documented, foreseeable, and forewarned outcome of deliberate choices made at the highest levels of power — choices that have now been placed before the International Court of Justice in a formal genocide case.

"The children of Gaza were born into a cage.

What began in 2023 was the door being locked — and the air turned off."
A Timeline of Events
1948 · Al-Nakba — The Catastrophe
700,000 Palestinians Expelled
During the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, over 700,000 Palestinians — around half the Arab population — were expelled from or fled their homes. More than 400 villages were destroyed or depopulated. The refugees and their descendants were not permitted to return. They were settled — largely without choice — in Gaza, the West Bank, Lebanon, Jordan, and Syria. Gaza received the largest concentration. The overcrowding, poverty, and statelessness that define Gaza today have their origin in this single catastrophic event.
1967 · Six-Day War
Gaza Placed Under Israeli Military Occupation
Following the Six-Day War, Israel occupied the Gaza Strip and the West Bank. Gaza was placed under Israeli military administration — controlling movement, construction, economy, and water. This occupation, and the legal obligations that come with it under international law, remains the foundation of Gaza's political status to this day. The United Nations considers it an occupied territory.
1993–2000 · Oslo Process
Limited Self-Governance — And Its Collapse
The Oslo Accords created the Palestinian Authority and established a framework for eventual Palestinian statehood. Gaza was to gain increasing self-governance. But the process stalled, settlements expanded in the West Bank, and the 2000 Camp David Summit broke down. The Second Intifada — years of bombings, assassinations, and military operations — followed. The promise of a two-state solution effectively collapsed.
2005 · Israeli Disengagement
Israeli Settlements Withdrawn — Control Maintained
Israel unilaterally withdrew its settlements and soldiers from inside Gaza. But it maintained full control of Gaza's land borders, airspace, territorial waters, electromagnetic spectrum, and population registry. The UN and international legal bodies have consistently found that Gaza remained under effective Israeli occupation even after disengagement.
2007 · The Blockade — 16 Years of Siege
Total Land, Sea & Air Blockade Imposed on 2.3 Million People
Following Hamas's takeover of Gaza and with international backing, Israel imposed a comprehensive blockade. Every item entering Gaza required Israeli approval. Entire categories of goods were banned. The blockade prevented the rebuilding of infrastructure destroyed in successive military operations — in 2008, 2012, 2014, and 2021. By 2022, the World Bank found that 60% of Gazans lived in poverty, 80% required food assistance, and the economy was in structural collapse. The blockade was declared illegal under international humanitarian law by multiple UN special rapporteurs.
October 7, 2023 · 06:29 local time
Hamas-Led Assault — 1,200 Israelis Killed, 253 Taken Hostage
Hamas and other armed groups broke through the Gaza perimeter fence in an early-morning assault. Approximately 1,200 Israelis were killed — the majority civilians — in the deadliest single day for Jewish people since the Holocaust. 253 people were taken hostage into Gaza, including women, children, and elderly. The attacks included documented atrocities at kibbutzim and the Nova music festival. The attacks were universally condemned as war crimes by international bodies. The Israeli government declared a state of war.
October 9, 2023 — Ongoing
Total Siege Declared. Bombing Campaign Begins.
Israel's Defence Minister declared a "complete siege" — no electricity, no food, no water, no fuel. Within days, an air and artillery bombardment campaign began at a scale and intensity without modern precedent in urban warfare. Within weeks, entire residential neighbourhoods, hospitals, schools, and refugee camps were destroyed. More than 2 million people were ordered to evacuate southward, then bombed in the south. The entire 2.3 million population of Gaza has been displaced at least once. By March 2026, over 50,000 Palestinians have been killed, with children making up more than 40% of documented deaths.
January 2024 · International Court of Justice
South Africa Files Genocide Case — ICJ Orders Provisional Measures
South Africa filed a case at the ICJ under the Genocide Convention, alleging that Israel's actions in Gaza constitute genocide. The ICJ issued provisional measures in January 2024, ordering Israel to prevent acts of genocide and to ensure humanitarian aid enters Gaza. The ICJ found it "plausible" that Palestinian rights under the Genocide Convention were at risk. The case continues. The provisional measures have not been fully implemented.
2024–2026 · Ongoing Destruction
The Crisis Continues to Deepen
Through 2024 and into 2026, the bombardment has continued with intermittent ceasefire negotiations. The famine declared in northern Gaza has spread south. Virtually every hospital has been damaged or destroyed. The entire school system has collapsed. An entire generation of children has grown up — or been killed — knowing nothing but war. The death toll continues to rise. The aid blockade has not been lifted to the levels humanitarian organisations say are required to prevent mass starvation.

How They Died

THE CAUSES THAT
TOOK THEIR LIVES

Verified causes drawn from UNICEF, WHO, and OCHA humanitarian reports. Each entry represents thousands of individual tragedies, each preventable under different circumstances.

01
💥 Primary Cause
💥
Airstrikes & Explosive Weapons
~70% of documented child deaths
The primary cause of child death. Aerial bombardment and artillery cause massive blast trauma, crush injuries from collapsed buildings, and shrapnel wounds to bodies too small to survive them. Many children remain under rubble, unreachable for days. The majority of verified deaths fall under this category — and experts believe the true figure is significantly higher.
Blast TraumaCrush InjuriesBurnsShrapnel
02
🏥 Medical Collapse
🏥
Collapse of Medical Care
Widespread preventable deaths
With over 50% of hospitals non-functional, children die from entirely treatable conditions. Premature newborns die when incubators lose power. Children with cancer lose chemotherapy access. Diabetic children run out of insulin. Surgeons operate by phone torchlight. A system built to heal has been systematically dismantled.
PreventableTreatable ConditionsNo Power
03
💧 Water Crisis
💧
Dehydration & Contaminated Water
Critical — especially under-5s
Destroyed water infrastructure forces families to rely on contaminated sources. Infants and children under 5 are biologically the most vulnerable. Severe dehydration combined with diarrheal disease becomes fatal within days without oral rehydration salts or IV fluids — neither of which are reliably available. Children die of thirst while surrounded by a sea.
Diarrhoeal DiseaseDehydrationSepsis

Rising rapidly — IPC Phase 5 confirmed
Children under 5 with Severe Acute Malnutrition face a 9× higher risk of death. Without therapeutic food, they deteriorate within weeks. IPC Phase 5 Famine conditions were declared in Northern Gaza — the highest emergency classification in the global hunger scale. Starving children die from infections that a healthy body would easily overcome.

🦠 Infectious Disease
🦠
Preventable Infectious Disease
Accelerating in displacement camps
Overcrowded displacement shelters with no sanitation are outbreak conditions. Hepatitis A, cholera, meningitis, and measles — diseases previously eliminated — are resurging. Malnourished children cannot mount an immune response. Every infection becomes potentially fatal in a body already compromised by hunger.
CholeraMeaslesHepatitis ARespiratory

🤱 Neonatal
🤱
Neonatal & Infant Mortality
Newborn deaths rising sharply
Pregnant women are giving birth without medical staff, anaesthesia, or sterile conditions. Premature newborns requiring incubators, oxygen, and IV nutrition die within hours. Neonatal sepsis without antibiotics is almost uniformly fatal. Some babies never take a breath in a world willing to fight for them.
Premature BirthNeonatal SepsisNo Medical Care

🆘
Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM)
~30,000+ children · 30× higher mortality risk
The most life-threatening form. A child with SAM has a weight-for-height ratio so low that vital organs begin to fail. The immune system collapses entirely — any infection, even a common cold, becomes fatal. Without Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food (RUTF) and medical treatment, up to 30% of SAM children die. In Gaza, RUTF stocks are critically depleted.
Muscle wastingOrgan failureImmune collapseOedemaMarasmusKwashiorkor
⚠️
Global Acute Malnutrition & Wasting
~65,000+ children · Rapid deterioration
Wasting occurs when a child's weight drops dangerously low relative to height — visible as loose skin, prominent ribs, sunken eyes. Without intervention, children with moderate wasting deteriorate to SAM within weeks. The body begins consuming its own muscle mass for energy.
Weight lossMuscle lossExtreme fatigueApathyWeakness
📉
Chronic Stunting
Long-term — permanent developmental damage
Stunting results from prolonged chronic malnutrition, especially in the first 1,000 days of life. It causes irreversible impairment to physical growth and cognitive development. Stunted children have lower brain volume, reduced IQ, and compromised learning capacity. It cannot be reversed — only prevented. Gaza's children are losing their futures in measurable, permanent ways.
Impaired growthCognitive delayBrain developmentIrreversible
🧬
Micronutrient Deficiencies
Widespread — "Hidden Hunger"
Severe shortages of Vitamin A, Zinc, Iodine, and Vitamin C. Vitamin A deficiency causes preventable blindness and dramatically increases mortality from measles. Scurvy — Vitamin C deficiency, eliminated from the developed world — is re-emerging for the first time in generations. Iodine deficiency permanently impairs brain development in young children.
Vitamin A deficiencyNight blindnessScurvyZinc deficiencyRickets
🩸
Iron-Deficiency Anaemia
Near-universal in young children & pregnant women
Anaemia is reaching near-universal levels. In infants, severe anaemia causes heart failure. In pregnant women, anaemia dramatically increases the risk of maternal death, premature birth, and low birth weight — perpetuating the cycle into the next generation before it even begins.

Root Causes

WHY CHILDREN ARE STARVING

The malnutrition crisis in Gaza is not a natural disaster. It has identifiable, documented causes.

🚫
Aid Blockade
Severe restrictions on humanitarian access have reduced food, medicine, and nutrition supplies to a fraction of what is needed. Aid convoys face systematic delays, denials, and attacks.
💸
Economic Collapse
90% of Gaza's population lives in extreme poverty. Bakeries have closed due to fuel shortages. Markets are empty. Families have no income and no food purchasing power whatsoever.
🌾
Agricultural Destruction
Gaza's farmland, greenhouses, and food production infrastructure have been systematically destroyed, eliminating local food sources that once supplemented humanitarian imports.
⚡
No Power, No Refrigeration
Electricity has been near-totally cut. Without power, food cannot be preserved, water cannot be desalinated, and hospitals cannot store vaccines, blood, or therapeutic nutrition supplies.
🏕️
Mass Displacement
Families repeatedly displaced and living in tents have no cooking facilities, no clean water, and are entirely dependent on aid that is not reaching them in the quantities required to sustain life.
👩‍⚕️
Collapsed Health System
Without functioning hospitals and nutrition specialists, children identified as malnourished cannot receive treatment even when aid organisations identify and try to reach them.
The Timeline of Starvation

WHAT HUNGER DOES
DAY BY DAY

Day 1–3 · Without Food
Glycogen Reserves Deplete
The body burns stored glucose. Children become lethargic, irritable, and unable to concentrate. Infants cry weakly. Blood sugar drops to dangerous levels.
Week 1–2 · Prolonged Starvation
Muscle Breakdown Begins
The body begins consuming muscle protein for energy. Visible wasting appears. Immune function drops sharply — any infection now becomes dangerous.
Week 3–6 · Severe Stage
Organ Stress & Kwashiorkor
Protein deficiency causes fluid retention — the swollen belly seen in severely malnourished children. The liver, kidneys, and heart begin to fail.
Month 2+ · Terminal Without Intervention
Multi-Organ Failure
Without intervention, the heart weakens, immunity collapses entirely, and death follows — often from an otherwise minor infection like diarrhoea or a simple cold.
WHAT IS RUTF?
🥜
Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food is a peanut-based paste enriched with vitamins and minerals. One sachet per day for 6–8 weeks can bring a child back from the edge of death. It requires no water, no cooking, no refrigeration. It saves lives. It is not reaching Gaza in sufficient quantities.

For Caregivers & Support Workers

GENTLE PRACTICES FOR CHILDREN

Evidence-informed, equipment-free techniques used by humanitarian child psychologists in crisis settings. Guideable by any trusted adult in any safe space.

Exercise 01 · Breathing
Belly Breathing
"Balloon Breath" — ages 4 and up
  • Sit comfortably; place both hands gently on the belly
  • Breathe in through nose for 4 counts — belly rises like a balloon
  • Hold softly for 2 counts
  • Breathe out through mouth for 6 counts — balloon deflates
  • Repeat 4–6 times. Notice the body growing calmer.
Exercise 02 · Breathing
Square Breathing
"Box Breath" — ages 6 and up
  • Sit upright, hands resting on knees
  • Breathe in through nose for 4 counts (side 1)
  • Hold for 4 counts (side 2)
  • Breathe out through mouth for 4 counts (side 3)
  • Hold for 4 counts (side 4). Repeat 4 rounds.
Exercise 03 · Movement
Child's Pose
"Seed Pose" — gentle yoga, all ages
  • Kneel on a comfortable surface, sit back on heels
  • Slowly fold upper body forward, forehead to ground
  • Arms rest forward or along sides — whatever feels right
  • Take 5–8 slow easy breaths; feel the back rise and fall
  • Rise slowly when ready — deeply calming for nervous system
Exercise 04 · Movement
Mountain & Star Pose
Grounding & energy — all ages
  • Stand with feet hip-width apart
  • Feel both feet firmly and steadily on the ground
  • Breathe in — raise both arms wide like a star
  • Hold for 3 breaths — strong and steady like a mountain
  • Breathe out and lower arms slowly. Repeat 3 times.
